The Science of Natural Language Acquisition

Krashen's Input Hypothesis: Your "i+1"

Our method is built on linguist Stephen Krashen's theory that we acquire language best when we receive "comprehensible input" that is just one step beyond our current level (i+1). Gummely’s AI ensures you’re always in this optimal learning zone—challenged, but never overwhelmed.

Optimal Lexical Coverage for Reading

Research shows that to comfortably read and understand a text, you need to know about 95-98% of the words. Our AI personalizes content to meet this threshold, allowing you to infer the meaning of new words from context, which dramatically improves retention.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Gummely based on? +

Gummely is built on Stephen Krashen's Input Hypothesis, which suggests language acquisition happens best when learners receive 'comprehensible input' (i+1).

How does Gummely personalize content? +

Our AI analyzes your vocabulary to deliver texts and chats where 90-98% of the words are already known to you, making new vocabulary acquisition intuitive.

Can I use Gummely offline? +

Currently, Gummely requires an internet connection for most features, including AI chat and content generation. Offline modes for downloaded content are under development.
